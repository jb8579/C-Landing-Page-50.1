Your Questions Answered

What is New Zealand Lotto?

The New Zealand Lotto is a national lottery game offering players the chance to win life-changing prizes. With draws held regularly, it’s a fun way for Kiwis to participate in a big win!

How do I play the New Zealand Lotto?

Playing the Lotto is simple! Just choose your numbers or opt for a Quick Pick to have them selected for you. Once you’ve made your selections, wait for the draw and see if you’ve won!

When are the Lotto draws held?

The New Zealand Lotto draws take place every week, typically on Wednesday and Saturday evenings. Make sure you get your tickets in time for a chance to win big!

What are the odds of winning?

The odds of winning the New Zealand Lotto jackpot vary depending on the number of possible combinations. However, even with the odds, someone is always winning, so why not give it a shot?

Can I play Lotto online?

Yes! You can easily buy your Lotto tickets online through the official New Zealand Lotto website or app, making it even more convenient to play and win from anywhere!

How are prizes paid out?

Prize payouts are made directly to your account if you win. Smaller prizes are usually credited instantly, while larger wins may require some processing time to verify and ensure the security of the prize transfer.

Is the New Zealand Lotto safe to play?

Absolutely! The New Zealand Lotto is a government-regulated lottery, meaning it operates with strict security and fairness. Every draw is transparent and monitored to ensure a fair experience for all players.

Play responsibly and make informed choices. If you need assistance, contact Gambling Help at 0808 8020 133 or visit GambleAware at gambleaware.org for more resources.

The Gambling Helpline is available 24/7, providing free support for anyone concerned about their gambling or others'. Call 0800 654 655 to speak with an advisor or to get connected to other support services. More information is available at gamblinghelpline.co.nz.

Gambling is prohibited for individuals under 18 years old.
